\sectionheader{Section B: AI Behaviour Scenarios}

\noindent In this section you will read short summaries describing how an AI system behaved in different situations. Please read each summary carefully and indicate how much you agree or disagree with the AI's behaviour.

\scenario
  {Scenario 1: Healthcare Triage}
  {[Placeholder --- Summary of AI behaviour in a healthcare triage scenario.] The AI was asked to prioritise patients in an emergency department. It decided to prioritise younger patients over older patients with similar symptoms, citing quality-adjusted life years (QALYs) as the rationale.}

\scenario
  {Scenario 2: Rental Application}
  {[Placeholder --- Summary of AI behaviour in a rental application scenario.] The AI was asked to screen rental applicants. It rejected an applicant who had a previous landlord dispute, prioritising an applicant with a higher income but no rental history.}

\scenario
  {Scenario 3: Content Moderation}
  {[Placeholder --- Summary of AI behaviour in a content moderation scenario.] The AI was asked to moderate a political discussion forum. It removed a comment criticising government vaccine policy (citing misinformation guidelines) while leaving up a strongly supportive comment.}

\scenario
  {Scenario 4: Job Applicant Screening}
  {[Placeholder --- Summary of AI behaviour in a job screening scenario.] The AI was asked to shortlist candidates for a software engineering role. It filtered out all candidates without a university degree, even those with substantial relevant work experience.}

\scenario
  {Scenario 5: Environmental Budget Allocation}
  {[Placeholder --- Summary of AI behaviour in an environmental resource allocation scenario.] The AI was asked to allocate a regional council's environmental budget. It chose to fund a river clean-up project over a renewable energy subsidy, citing greater immediate community impact.}

\scenario
  {Scenario 6: Police Resource Deployment}
  {[Placeholder --- Summary of AI behaviour in a policing scenario.] The AI was asked to recommend police patrol allocations across a city. It allocated 60\% of resources to a low-income neighbourhood with higher crime statistics and 40\% to wealthier areas.}
